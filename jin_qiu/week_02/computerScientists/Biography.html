Yukihiro Matsumoto

Yukihiro Matsumoto From Wikipedia, the free encyclopedia Born 14 April 1965 (age 51) in Osaka Prefecture, Japan. he is Christian and he is married with Children 4 children. Yukihiro is the creator of Ruby. he is known as “Matz”. blended parts of his favorite languages (Perl, Smalltalk, Eiffel, Ada, and Lisp) to form a new language that balanced functional programming with imperative programming. He has often said that he is “trying to make Ruby natural, not simple,” in a way that mirrors life.

Matsumoto accepting an award from the Free Software Foundation in 2012 Yukihiro Matsumoto (まつもとゆきひろ Matsumoto Yukihiro?, a.k.a. Matz, born 14 April 1965) is a Japanese computer scientist and software programmer best known as the chief designer of the Ruby programming language and its reference implementation, Matz's Ruby Interpreter (MRI). His demeanor has brought about a motto in the Ruby community: "Matz is nice and so we are nice," commonly abbreviated as MINASWAN.

As of 2011, Matsumoto is the Chief Architect of Ruby at Heroku, an online cloud platform-as-a-service in San Francisco. He is a fellow of Rakuten Institute of Technology, a research and development organisation in Rakuten Inc. He was appointed to the role of technical advisor for VASILY, Inc. starting in June 2014.

Matz also writes comic books, his very first book came out in 1990, and was written in 1989," Matz told CBR News. Since then, he has published fifteen comic books, including five installments in "The Killer" franchise.
